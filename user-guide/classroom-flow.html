Classroom Flow

You can switch to Classroom mode using the mode dropdown in the top-left of the page and selecting “Classroom”. Below is the step-by-step flow with screenshots.

Step 1 — Data input (first page)​

  • You land on the Data Input page first.
  • Select a data source. By default, 80% of the data will be used to train the model and 20% to test it.
  • Options include preprocessed Kepler and TESS datasets, or you can upload your own (an example format is provided).

Step 2 — Dataset overview and target​

  • Scroll to see a full overview of the selected or uploaded dataset.
  • Choose the Target column (the value the model will predict) from a dropdown.

Step 3 — Select features​

  • Choose which features (columns) to include for training.
  • When ready, click the “Select Model” button at the bottom-right.

Step 4 — Choose and configure a model​

  • Pick the type of model to train: Neural Network or Random Forest.
  • Configure settings appropriate to your choice (e.g., layers/epochs for NN; trees/depth for RF).
  • Click “Train Model” when your configuration is set.

Step 5 — Training page​

  • You’ll be taken to the Training page.
  • Click “Start Training” when you’re ready.

Step 6 — Training progress​

  • Watch training progress with interactive visualizations.
  • After training completes, either “Adjust & Retrain” to refine, or choose “Go to Test & Export”.

Step 7 — Test model​

  • You’ll arrive at the Test & Export page.
  • Click “Test Model” to evaluate using the 20% test split.

Step 8 — Review results and export​

  • View full analytics of the test run with interactive visualizations.
  • Click “Export Trained Model” to download your model.
  • To start over, click “Train Another Model”.
